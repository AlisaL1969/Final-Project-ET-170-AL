We were great with our five cats: Nala, Merry, Pippin, Gali and Misty for a few months, until a huge black and white tomcat decided to start hanging out on our front stoop. He was friendly, but obviously didn't have a home. We think he was dumped by previous owners. We started calling him Mongo. He had a very bad cut on his back paw, and would limp. It wasn't getting better because he was living outdoors. Our five cats didn't like him on our stoop, and would growl and hiss at him through the screen door. Right before the winter of 2017-2018, we caught him and brought him to the vet. They neutered him, gave him shots, fixed his foot, and let us know he was FIV-positive (the feline eqivalent of AIDS). He now lives in our basement since November, 2017 because he can't live with the other five. He had a girlfriend (a tiny, black and white feral female) outside who was always around, and he would share the food we gave him with her. We knew she had given birth to at least 3 litters of kittens, because our neighbors had found good homes for them all. This mother cat was elusive and hard to catch. My neighbor finally caught her, took her to the vet and had her spayed. She came to live with us (down in the basement with Mongo) and while still showing feral, she is slowly coming out of her shell. We call her Mama. There is a high possibility that Mama is Misty's mom, and a chance that Mongo is Misty's dad!
